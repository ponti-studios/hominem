# Roadmap

## Direction

The product direction is to deepen Hakumi as a notes-first personal workspace rather than expand it into a suite of loosely connected apps.

The roadmap should strengthen the core loop of capture, organization, retrieval, and action.

## Now

The immediate focus is to make the core workspace feel complete and coherent.

- clarify the product story and terminology
- make the unified feed the clear home surface
- strengthen the relationship between notes and chat
- improve capture quality across writing and voice
- make the product feel calm, legible, and native across platforms

## Next

The next stage is to make the workspace smarter without making it heavier.

- better contextual linking between notes and chats
- stronger retrieval through chat over personal context
- smoother transitions between quick capture and deep work
- clearer platform roles between mobile capture and desktop or web synthesis
- lightweight action-taking that grows naturally from notes and chat context

## Later

Longer term, the product can expand only if expansion strengthens the core workspace rather than fragmenting it.

Possible areas include:

- richer memory and retrieval systems
- more proactive context surfacing
- collaboration or sharing around selected notes
- adjacent domains that genuinely benefit from the same shared context

These should be evaluated against one standard: do they make the notes-first workspace more valuable, or do they pull the product back toward a multi-app suite.

## Open questions

### Brand

The product direction is clearer than the brand.

Open questions:

- whether `Hakumi` is the long-term name
- how the brand should express calmness, intelligence, and continuity
- whether the current name supports the product's notes-first point of view

See [Naming](/Users/charlesponti/Developer/hominem/docs/naming.md) for the current recommendation and criteria.

### Product framing

`Notes-first personal workspace` is the current working framing.

It is strong because it keeps notes central while leaving room for AI and action. It should remain the default framing unless a better phrase clarifies the same idea without broadening the product too much.

### Scope discipline

The repo still contains traces of a broader life-management platform.

The ongoing product question is not whether those domains exist technically. It is whether they belong in the primary product story. Until they clearly reinforce the workspace model, they should remain outside the core narrative.
