# Vision

## Product definition

Hakumi is a notes-first personal workspace.

It helps people capture thoughts quickly, organize them into durable context, and use chat to think with what they already know. The product is not a collection of separate life-management apps. It is one workspace with one memory, one entry point, and one evolving body of context.

## Why this product exists

People do not struggle to create information. They struggle to keep it alive.

Ideas get captured in fragments: a quick voice note, half a written thought, a follow-up question, a task hidden inside a paragraph. Over time, those fragments scatter across tools and lose their usefulness. The cost is not just mess. It is repeated thinking, lost momentum, and the inability to build on prior insight.

Hakumi exists to turn scattered thought into usable context.

## Who it is for

Hakumi is for people who think in fragments and want one place where those fragments can become something coherent.

That includes:

- people who write notes to think, not just to archive
- people who capture ideas on the move and return to them later
- people who want AI to work from their real context, not from a blank prompt
- people who want one calm workspace instead of a bundle of loosely connected tools

## Core problem

Most tools make people choose too early between capture, organization, and action.

- capture tools are fast, but shallow
- organization tools are powerful, but demanding
- chat tools are flexible, but forgetful

Hakumi should remove that tradeoff. Capture comes first. Structure can emerge later. Chat should help make sense of the resulting context rather than becoming a separate destination.

## Product thesis

The product thesis is simple:

- one workspace beats many apps
- notes are the primary unit of personal knowledge
- chat is a way to think with notes, not a separate product
- the best system starts with low-friction capture and compounds into better retrieval and action

## What makes Hakumi distinct

Hakumi should feel:

- calm instead of crowded
- notes-first instead of assistant-first
- context-rich instead of prompt-dependent
- unified instead of modular
- useful in motion and at rest

The product should reward quick capture in the moment and deeper thinking later, using the same underlying context both times.

## Brand status

The current name is Hakumi. It is serviceable as a working product name, but the brand story is still unresolved.

The documentation should assume:

- the product direction is stable
- the name may still change
- the product should be defined by its behavior and point of view rather than by etymology
